Bakht by Mehrunisa Shahmeer

Description

Bakht by Mehrunisa Shahmeer is a captivating Urdu novel that explores themes of love, loss, and societal expectations. The story revolves around Halay, a spirited young woman navigating the complexities of life. Her journey is intertwined with the lives of Umer Hayat and Haroon, creating a compelling narrative filled with passion, heartbreak, and resilience. Through vivid characters and intricate plotlines, the author delves into the depths of human emotions, offering readers a thought-provoking and engaging reading experience

Review:

I've been seeing a lot of hype around Bakht by Mehrunisa Shahmeer, so I decided to give it a shot. Honestly, it was a mixed bag for me. The book is a mix of emotions, thatâ€™s for sure.
The story started off strong. I was really into Halay's character. Halay, the main character, is a strong woman, but sometimes her decisions were like, "Girl, what are you thinking?" I get that she's going through a lot, but some of her choices were just...wild.

She's feisty and independent, which is refreshing. But as the story progressed, I felt like it dragged a bit. Some parts felt repetitive, and I found myself skipping pages.Umer Hayat, oh boy, he's a complex character. I liked him, hated him, then liked him again. It was a rollercoaster. Haroon, well, he's the typical good guy, you know?

The love triangle was okay, I guess. It's a classic trope, but it worked here. Umer Hayat is definitely a complex character, and I couldn't quite figure him out. Haroon, well, he's the safe choice. The ending was a surprise, I'll give it that. It wasn't what I expected at all. But it left me with a lot of questions. The story is good, don't get me wrong, but it dragged a bit in the middle. I found myself skimming through a few parts. The ending was okay, I guess. Not exactly what I expected.
